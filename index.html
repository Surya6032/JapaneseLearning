Combination characters (拗音 youon) are formed by pairing an i-row character with a small や, ゆ, or よ. The small kana shrinks the sound into one syllable.

💡 Rule: Write the base character + small や / ゆ / よ side by side. Together they make one sound: きゃ = kya (not ki-ya).

These special rules affect how certain characters are written and read. Mastering them will make your reading much smoother.

1. Small っ / ッ — Double Consonant (Geminate)
A small tsu (っ/ッ) means the next consonant is doubled. It acts as a short pause.
Example: きって (kitte) = stamp  |  ざっし (zasshi) = magazine  |  マッチ (matchi) = match
2. Long Vowels — Hiragana
In hiragana, long vowels are written by adding the matching vowel:
oo → おお or おう (e.g. おおきい = ōkii = big)  |  uu → うう  |  aa → ああ
3. Long Vowels — Katakana
In katakana, a long vowel is shown with a dash: ー
Example: コーヒー (kōhī) = coffee  |  ケーキ (kēki) = cake  |  テーブル (tēburu) = table
4. は and へ as Particles
は (normally "ha") is read as "wa" when used as the topic marker particle.
へ (normally "he") is read as "e" when used as the direction particle.
Example: わたしは がくせいです。(watashi wa gakusei desu.) = I am a student.
5. ん / ン — Standalone N
ん / ン never starts a word. It always follows another syllable.
Before b/m/p sounds it may sound like m: さんぽ (sampo) = walk.
Example: にほん (nihon) = Japan  |  てんき (tenki) = weather

Yesterday, while I was heading to school on the train, an elderly woman got on. All the seats were taken and she was standing. Next month, I am planning to travel to Kyoto with a friend. Kyoto is Japan's ancient capital and has many temples and shrines. First, we intend to go see Kinkakuji. Japan's Seasons

Japan has four seasons: spring, summer, autumn, and winter. In spring, cherry blossoms bloom and it is very beautiful. In summer it is hot and many people go to the sea or mountains. In autumn the leaves turn red and the autumn foliage is beautiful. In winter it is cold and some regions get snow. I like autumn the most. It is because it is cool and the food is delicious. I particularly look forward to saury fish and chestnuts. Depending on the season, Japan's nature shows many different faces.

But the teacher said with a smile, "Let's enjoy it even in the rain. Who brought a raincoat?" The children put on their raincoats and headed to the park. In the rain, everyone played in puddles and found snails. "Rainy days are fun too!" the children shouted. The warm udon noodles they ate at the cafeteria for lunch tasted much better than usual. Even when things are different from what you expected, you can find a different kind of fun. The children thought about this after returning to school.

A Letter from Grandfather

Haruka sent her grandfather news of her university acceptance. A little while later, a letter arrived from her grandfather. Inside it were these words: "Haruka, congratulations on getting in. At university I want you to study hard and meet many people. There will be difficult things, but don't give up — keep going. Grandpa will always be cheering you on." As Haruka read the letter, tears came to her eyes. Her grandfather lives far away and she can't see him often, but she felt that he was always in her heart.
